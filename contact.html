The Ginkyo of Poetry and Ceremony (Lu)

Peotry and Ceremony are valued by Confucianism, when the dish is invented, the Ginkyo is from the trees outside of a Confucianism peotry hall.

Dong Po Meat (Zhe)

The dish is invented by a great poet Su Dongpo, who used this meat to reward worker who helped with water projects in Hangzhou

Song Sao Fish Soup (Zhe)

When the Song emperor visted the West lake, he was impressed by the Fish Soup made by a woman with Song as last name. The emperor granted much gold and jewelry to Song and the dish became famous.

Shadow Beef (Chuan)

The dish inspired by a traditional art that uses shadows and silhoutte to present dramas. The beef is so nice that it can be sliced so thin that you can see through the meat with light.

Yipin Pot (Hui)

Yipin is an official title, and when the Ming emporor visited an Yipin officer, his wife cooked this food and the emperor was fascinated by the taste, giving the dish a name of Yipin.

Braised meatball(Su)

The direct translation of the dish is "lion head". The story behind the name is that when a general hosted a banquet, his friends praised his bravery and exploit, so powerful as a lion. Thus, they decided to call the meatball dish they were eating as "Lion Head".

Ningyuan Tofu (Xiang)

The dish is made by putting meat inside tofu. It was said that once two brothers couldn't get along with each other, and one day, one of them bought tofu while the other bought meat. Their mother made this dish to teach them collaborating as meat and tofu will produce better results than only one.

Ding Bian Hu (Min)

The name directly translates as "paste beside the vessel". The dish was said to be invented by a housewife who didn't have enough rice while accommodating guests, so she poured rice milk around the pot in which she was cooking other courses. The rice milk solidified and was very tasty.

BBQ Pork (Yue)

The name of BBQ Pork in Chinese means "stick cooking". The dish is made by cooking sliced tenderloin on a stick.

Sweet Lotus Root (Su)

The dish is made by filling the holes in lotus root with sticky rice, and use syrup to sweeten. It is one of the most famous street food in the Su regions.
