About

Su is the abbreviation for Jiangsu province, which is on the east coast of China. During the Tang and Song dynasty, the economy of the Su region grew rapidly, driving the prosperity of the food industry. Su is by the side of a canal and the Yangze River, its good geographical location has also made Su Cuisine famous around the globe.

Specialties and Features

The Su region enjoys nice freshwater fish from the rivers crossing by, and the weather is also nice enough to grow various vegetables. Su chefs are specifically good at braising and emphasize on the balance and nature of ingredients. The dish layouts also pursue an elegant style. The traditional "ManHanQuanXi", which translates to "the Han's Feast" is also mainly based on Su Cuisine and is shown above.
