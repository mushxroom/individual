About

Lu is the abbreviation for Shandong province, which is on the northeast coast of China. Shandong is a historical province with a profound history and is one of the origins of Chinese culture. The cuisines of Lu is directed by Confusionism and values delicacy, health, and balance. It is the most difficult to cook style of cuisine with the most various skills and longest history.

Specialties and Features

With its fertile land and a long coastline, people of Lu are equipped with nice materials as the base for delicious dishes. The Lu chefs pursue fresh, crispy, tender, and mild dishes, emphasizing on the original taste of the ingredients. The use of fire and timing is extremely important in Lu dishes. In addition, the layout and display of the dishes are also greatly affected by courtesy of Confusionism, focusing on gaudiness and etiquette. There are also many Lu standard banquets for various occasions.
