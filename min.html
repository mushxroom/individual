About

Min is the abbreviation for Fujian province, which is on the northeast coast of China. The splendid culture is a mixture of Han culture in the central plain of China and the Minyue culture in the south coast of China. Since Min people has strong bond with the ocean and have open characters, they also eat a large variety of food. They are especially good at making various soup and soup based dishes.

Specialties and Features

By the ocean, Min chefs are good at cooking seafood with high steaming, stir-frying, and braising techniques. In addition, the Min area is mountainous, so chefs are also good at using mushrooms, bamboo shoots, and other ingredients from the mountains. Min chefs are especially good at knife skills and can produce extremely thin slices. The soup pictured above is called "Fotiaoqiang", which translates to "the buddist coming from the other side of the wall" to show the nice smell of the dish. This dish is also used in banquets of president meetings.
